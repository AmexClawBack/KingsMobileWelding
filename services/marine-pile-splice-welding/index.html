Common Marine Pile Welding Scopes

Marine pile welding may involve field splices, extensions, pile caps, bracing, templates, pile repairs, support steel, or welding tied to docks, piers, bulkheads, bridges, ports, terminals, and waterfront foundations.

  • Pipe pile splices: steel pipe pile field splices, extensions, large-diameter pile, dock pile, pier pile, bridge pile, and waterfront foundation pile.
  • H-pile splices: driven H-pile splices, pile extensions, pile repairs, bridge foundations, dock and pier supports, and pile cap-related work.
  • Pile caps and bracing: contractor-directed welding support for caps, braces, templates, connection plates, stiffeners, and field modifications.
  • Marine foundation welding: welding support for piles tied to docks, piers, bulkheads, seawalls, wharves, ports, terminals, and waterfront industrial work.
  • Bridge-adjacent pile work: pile welding support where AWS D1.5, DOT specifications, owner requirements, or inspection-driven work may apply.
  • Emergency pile repair support: urgent field welding review for damaged pile steel, failed support steel, impact damage, or schedule-critical repairs.

Important Pile Welding Planning Notes

Pile splices and pile-related welding can affect structural capacity, pile capacity, foundation performance, bridge requirements, DOT requirements, and inspection acceptance. The welding scope should be tied to the project drawings, specifications, engineer detail, or responsible contractor direction.

  • Confirm whether the work falls under AWS D1.1, AWS D1.5, DOT specifications, owner specifications, or project-specific welding requirements.
  • Repair details and splice details should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, marine contractor, pile driving contractor, owner, inspector, DOT authority, or qualified authority when required.
  • Access, crane support, pile position, waterline, tides, weather, barge access, and inspection hold points can affect the welding plan.
  • We perform contractor-directed and owner-directed field welding support, not full engineering, permitting, diving, or complete marine construction.

What to Send for a Marine Pile Splice Welding Quote

The fastest way to get accurate pricing is to send the pile details, splice requirements, access information, inspection requirements, and project schedule up front.

For pile driving and marine work, the welding itself is only one part of the scope. Sequencing, crane access, work windows, inspection, safety, and site access all affect the job.

Send These Details

  • Project location, contractor name, site contact, required start date, and anticipated work window
  • Pipe OD, wall thickness, H-pile size, pile length, pile schedule, splice count, and splice location
  • Drawings, splice details, weld symbols, engineer details, DOT requirements, owner requirements, or marked-up plans
  • Photos of the pile, work area, access route, crane/lift location, waterline, barge setup, or surrounding site conditions
  • Whether piles are vertical, battered, over water, at a dock, inside a cofferdam, near a bulkhead, or on a bridge/marine project
  • Inspection requirements, AWS requirements, DOT requirements, WPS requirements, weld testing requirements, or hold points
  • Access restrictions, crane or lift support, barge access, tide restrictions, weather limitations, parking, work hours, and marine safety rules
  • COI, W-9, safety information, certification information, procurement, or onboarding requirements
