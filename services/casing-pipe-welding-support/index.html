Casing & Pipe Applications

  • Drilled shaft casing: casing sections, permanent casing, temporary casing, caisson casing, and deep foundation casing splices
  • Bore casing and utility casing: steel casing used for utility, roadway, rail, drainage, and infrastructure work
  • Pipe pile and foundation pipe: pipe pile splice welding, large-diameter pipe pile, bridge pile, marine pile, and support pipe

Field Conditions We Commonly Work Around

  • Restricted access: bridge sites, rail sites, utility corridors, marine work, industrial facilities, and active heavy civil jobsites

What to Send for Fast Casing Pipe Welding Pricing

Casing pipe and field splice scopes price faster when the material, splice detail, access conditions, and inspection requirements are clear up front.

Send These Details

  • Project location, state, site contact, target start date, and expected duration
  • Drawings, splice details, casing details, pipe details, weld symbols, WPS requirements, or marked-up plans
  • Photos of the casing, pipe, pile, splice area, work zone, access conditions, or existing field issue
  • Casing or pipe size, outside diameter, wall thickness, steel grade if known, joint configuration, and number of splices
  • Scope type: casing splice, drilled shaft casing, bore casing, utility casing, pipe pile splice, support steel, repair, extension, or modification
  • Access constraints including crane access, lift access, low-headroom conditions, confined work zones, lane closures, rail access, marine access, shutdown windows, or night work
  • Inspection requirements, DOT requirements, QC/QA notes, coating requirements, project specifications, and schedule constraints

Casing Pipe Welding Support FAQ

Do you provide casing pipe welding support in the field?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides mobile field welding support for casing pipe, steel casing, drilled shaft casing, bore casing, utility casing, temporary casing, permanent casing, pipe pile, and related heavy civil welding scopes.

Can you support casing pipe suppliers and steel pipe suppliers?

Yes. We support casing pipe suppliers, steel pipe suppliers, pipe piling suppliers, and fabrication companies when their customers need casing, pile, or pipe sections spliced, modified, repaired, or welded in the field.

Do you weld drilled shaft casing?

Yes. We support drilled shaft casing welding, permanent casing splices, temporary casing support, caisson-related welding, and deep foundation field welding for foundation drilling contractors, deep foundation contractors, and heavy civil project teams.

Do you provide pipe pile splice welding?

Yes. We provide pipe pile splice welding and field pile splice welding for pile driving contractors, deep foundation contractors, steel pipe suppliers, bridge contractors, marine contractors, and infrastructure teams. See our pipe pile splice welding and field pile splice welding pages.

Can you support bore casing and utility casing welding?

Yes. We can support contractor-directed bore casing, utility casing, steel casing, support pipe, casing extensions, casing repairs, and field modifications where the welding requirements and project details are provided by the contractor, engineer, or project documents.

Do you support heavy civil and DOT-related casing work?

Yes. We support casing, pipe, pile, foundation, bridge, rail, utility, marine, industrial, and heavy civil field welding scopes across Virginia, Maryland, D.C., North Carolina, South Carolina, and the Mid-Atlantic.

Do you engineer casing splice details?

No. Kings Mobile Welding performs contractor-directed and owner-directed welding. When welding affects structural capacity, pile capacity, casing requirements, bridge components, DOT requirements, code-controlled work, life safety, or engineered systems, the splice detail or repair detail should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, project specifications, or qualified authority.

What do you need to quote casing pipe welding support?

The fastest quote comes from drawings or splice details, casing size, pipe size, wall thickness, steel grade if known, number of splices, photos, project location, access conditions, crane or lift access, inspection requirements, and required start date. Start by sending the scope through our contact page.
