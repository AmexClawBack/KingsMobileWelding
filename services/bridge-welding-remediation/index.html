AWS D1.5 Bridge Welding FAQ

Do bridge projects typically require AWS D1.5 welders?

Many welded bridge scopes reference the AASHTO/AWS D1.5 Bridge Welding Code along with DOT-specific project requirements. If your plans or specifications call for AWS D1.5, AWS certified bridge welders, or qualified bridge welders, Kings Mobile Welding can support the project with field welding aligned to those requirements and inspection expectations.

Are your welders NCDOT certified for bridge work in North Carolina?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding has NCDOT certified welders available for bridge work in North Carolina. This allows us to support bridge contractors, deep foundation contractors, and infrastructure contractors working on North Carolina bridge and DOT projects.

Can you support pile splice welding on bridge and deep foundation projects?

Yes. We provide field welding support for H-pile splice welding, pipe pile splice welding, permanent shaft casing splices, and related bridge foundation steel where welding is specified. Send the splice detail, material information, location, access conditions, inspection requirements, and schedule through our contact page for pricing and availability.

Do you perform bridge expansion joint welding?

Yes. We provide field welding support for bridge expansion joint systems, including steel edge rails, armor angles, embedded plates, elastomeric expansion dam systems, and related bridge joint repair and replacement work tied to maintenance and rehabilitation scopes.

What states do you support for bridge welding and mobile welding?

Kings Mobile Welding supports qualified bridge, DOT, pile splice, and infrastructure welding scopes across Virginia mobile welding, Maryland mobile welding, D.C. mobile welding, North Carolina mobile welding, and South Carolina mobile welding.

What do you need to quote AWS D1.5 bridge welding work?

The fastest quote comes from drawings or splice details, photos of existing conditions, project location, access constraints, traffic control or shutdown windows, inspection requirements, and required start dates. Start on our contact page.

Do you coordinate with inspectors and QC/QA?

Yes. Bridge work is commonly inspection-driven. We coordinate with superintendents, QC, QA, engineers, and inspection staff for hold points, access readiness, documentation, and closeout requirements to help reduce rework and maintain schedule.

Need AWS Certified Bridge Welders or AWS D1.5 Welders?

Send drawings, splice details, photos, project location, access notes, DOT requirements, inspection requirements, and traffic window constraints. We will review the scope and confirm availability for bridge welding support.
