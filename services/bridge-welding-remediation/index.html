AWS D1.5 Bridge Welding FAQ

Do bridge projects typically require AWS D1.5 welders?

Many welded bridge scopes reference the AASHTO/AWS D1.5 Bridge Welding Code along with DOT-specific project requirements. If your plans or specifications call for AWS D1.5, AWS certified bridge welders, or qualified bridge welders, Kings Mobile Welding can support the project with field welding aligned to those requirements and inspection expectations.

Are your welders NCDOT certified for bridge work in North Carolina?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding has NCDOT certified welders available for bridge work in North Carolina. This allows us to support bridge contractors, deep foundation contractors, and infrastructure contractors working on North Carolina bridge and DOT projects.

Do you provide bridge diaphragm and cross-frame welding?

Yes. We provide field welding support for bridge diaphragms, cross-frames, bracing steel, diaphragm connection plates, replacement steel, and engineered repair details. See our bridge diaphragm and cross-frame welding page for more details.

Do you support bridge stiffener and connector plate repairs?

Yes. We support bridge stiffener plate repair, connector plate replacement, diaphragm connection plate welding, bearing stiffener repairs, repair plates, gussets, brackets, and related DOT bridge remediation work. See our bridge stiffener and connector plate repair page.

Can you weld steel bridge girder and beam end repairs?

Yes. We support steel bridge girder repair welding, beam end repair welding, web repairs, flange repairs, bearing area repairs, repair plates, stiffeners, and engineer-directed remediation details. See our steel bridge girder and beam end repair welding page.

Do you provide welding support for bridge bearing pad replacement?

Yes. We provide welding support for bridge bearing pad replacement scopes, including bearing plates, sole plates, masonry plates, anchor bolt-related steel repairs, girder bearing areas, and jacking-coordinated bridge repair work. See our bridge bearing pad replacement welding support page.

Can you support pile splice welding on bridge and deep foundation projects?

Yes. We provide field welding support for H-pile splice welding, pipe pile splice welding, permanent shaft casing splices, and related bridge foundation steel where welding is specified. Send the splice detail, material information, location, access conditions, inspection requirements, and schedule through our contact page for pricing and availability.

Do you perform bridge expansion joint welding?

Yes. We provide field welding support for bridge expansion joint systems, including steel edge rails, armor angles, embedded plates, elastomeric expansion dam systems, and related bridge joint repair and replacement work tied to maintenance and rehabilitation scopes.

What do you need to quote AWS D1.5 bridge welding work?

The fastest quote comes from drawings or splice details, photos of existing conditions, project location, access constraints, traffic control or shutdown windows, inspection requirements, and required start dates. For component-specific bridge steel repairs, include photos and details for diaphragms, cross-frames, stiffeners, connector plates, girders, beam ends, bearing areas, expansion joints, or pile splices. Do you coordinate with inspectors and QC/QA?

Yes. Bridge work is commonly inspection-driven. We coordinate with superintendents, QC, QA, engineers, steel crews, jacking contractors, and inspection staff for hold points, access readiness, documentation, weld sequencing, and closeout requirements to help reduce rework and maintain schedule.
