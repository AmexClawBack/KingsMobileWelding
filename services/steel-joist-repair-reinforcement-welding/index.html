Steel Joist Repair & Reinforcement Welding FAQ

Do you provide steel joist repair welding?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides field welding support for steel joist repairs, bar joist repairs, chord repairs, web member repairs, reinforcement plates, reinforcement angles, failed weld repairs, and approved joist repair details.

Do you provide bar joist reinforcement welding?

Yes. We support bar joist reinforcement welding for commercial buildings, renovations, rooftop unit support, HVAC upgrades, roof opening changes, load-related reinforcement, added plates, and added angles when shown on approved drawings or directed by the project team.

Can you weld reinforcement for rooftop units or HVAC equipment?

Yes. We support welding for rooftop unit support steel, HVAC support frames, equipment support framing, joist reinforcement, roof opening support, and field-installed support details based on approved drawings, repair details, or field instructions.

Do steel joist repairs need to be engineered?

Many steel joist repair and reinforcement scopes should be designed or approved by a project engineer, responsible design professional, or joist manufacturer. We perform the welding portion based on approved drawings, weld symbols, repair details, project specifications, or field instructions.

Do you provide AWS D1.1 welding for steel joist repairs?

Yes. We support AWS D1.1 structural steel field welding when steel joist repair or reinforcement details require qualified welding.

Can you repair joist chords and web members?

Yes. We support welding for top chord repairs, bottom chord repairs, web member repairs, web reinforcement, replacement angles, added plates, added bracing, and approved joist repair details.

What areas do you serve for steel joist repair welding?

We support qualified steel joist repair and reinforcement welding projects across Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Maryland, and Washington D.C..

What do you need to quote a steel joist repair?

Send drawings, repair details, photos, weld symbols, project location, access notes, inspection requirements, and schedule constraints. Photos of the joist, chord, web member, rooftop equipment, support frame, failed weld, or repair area are especially helpful.
