Common Fender System & Dolphin Welding Scopes

Fender and dolphin repair welding often starts after vessel impact, equipment impact, storm damage, or routine inspection that identifies damaged brackets, cracked steel, missing plates, bent support components, or failed attachments.

  • Fender support steel welding: brackets, plates, welded attachments, support steel, repair plates, and field modifications.
  • Marine fender repair support: steel repair work tied to dock fenders, wharf fenders, terminal fenders, and protection systems.
  • Dolphin repair support: contractor-directed welding support for breasting dolphins, mooring dolphins, brackets, plates, and pile-related support steel.
  • Impact damage repairs: vessel impact, barge impact, equipment impact, storm damage, bent steel, cracked steel, and failed weld areas.
  • Port and terminal repairs: fender support steel, access platforms, ladders, rails, brackets, repair plates, and waterfront industrial steel.
  • Emergency repair welding: urgent repair support for damaged fender systems, failed brackets, and schedule-critical berth or dock repair work.

Important Fender & Dolphin Repair Notes

Fender systems and dolphins may be tied to berthing loads, mooring loads, pile capacity, wharf structures, dock protection, port operations, and owner-controlled facilities. Welding repair details should be verified when structural performance or vessel contact is involved.

  • Repair details should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, marine contractor, owner, port authority, terminal authority, inspector, or qualified authority when structural performance is involved.
  • Berthing areas, active terminals, vessel traffic, tides, waterline exposure, and access conditions can affect how the repair is planned.
  • Some repairs may require owner approval, terminal coordination, safety review, engineering direction, or inspection before welding begins.
  • We perform contractor-directed and owner-directed field welding support, not full engineering, permitting, diving, or complete marine construction.

What to Send for a Fender System or Dolphin Welding Quote

The fastest way to get accurate pricing is to send photos and project details before requesting a site visit. For fender systems and dolphins, the repair location, damage type, access, and owner requirements matter as much as the weld itself.

Send close-up photos, wide-angle photos, repair drawings if available, and notes about the berth, dock, pier, wharf, waterline, tide window, access route, and whether the facility is active.

Send These Details

  • Project location, port/terminal/marina/facility name, site contact, and required repair timing
  • Photos of the fender system, dolphin, bracket, plate, support steel, pile, fender support, or damaged steel
  • Wide-angle photos showing berth area, waterline, dock/pier/wharf layout, equipment access, and surrounding work area
  • What caused the damage, if known: vessel impact, barge impact, equipment impact, storm damage, corrosion, or failed bracket
  • Steel thickness, bracket dimensions, plate sizes, pipe/pile size, fender system details, or repair component details if known
  • Engineer drawings, repair sketches, owner requirements, terminal requirements, inspection requirements, or manufacturer details if available
  • Access restrictions, lift/crane access, barge access, tide restrictions, parking, work hours, and marine safety rules
  • COI, W-9, safety information, certification information, procurement, or onboarding requirements
