Fixed Ladder Installation Should Be Planned Before It Gets Bolted to the Building

A commercial fixed ladder is not just a piece of steel on a wall. The ladder height, roof transition, wall construction, standoff distance, anchors, fall protection, parapet condition, inspection requirements, and access path all matter.

  • Wall conditions matter: CMU, brick, concrete, steel framing, parapets, siding, and existing structure can change the attachment method.
  • Fall protection matters: taller ladders may require a ladder safety system or personal fall arrest system depending on the project and applicable requirements.
  • Access matters: lifts, parking, tenant access, roof access, after-hours work, power lines, landscaping, and loading zones can affect scheduling.
  • Details matter: the ladder manufacturer, engineer, owner, project drawings, building code, and OSHA requirements should be reviewed before installation.

Fixed Ladder Installation FAQ

Do you install fixed ladders on commercial buildings?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding installs fixed ladders for commercial buildings, retail properties, industrial facilities, property management projects, roof access, equipment access, exterior wall access, and contractor-directed build-out scopes.

Do you install roof access ladders?

Yes. We install commercial roof access ladders, exterior fixed ladders, wall-mounted steel ladders, replacement ladders, and access ladders for rooftops, mechanical equipment, parapets, mezzanines, platforms, and maintenance areas.

Can you replace an existing fixed ladder?

Yes. We can support fixed ladder replacement when an existing ladder is rusted, damaged, outdated, improperly installed, or no longer fits the building’s access needs. Replacement details should be reviewed against owner, code, manufacturer, engineer, and inspection requirements.

Can you weld and install fixed ladder brackets?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding can perform field welding, bracket installation, steel support installation, ladder attachment welding, and contractor-directed anchorage support. Anchors, attachment details, and structural requirements should be verified by the responsible engineer, manufacturer, project specifications, building owner, or qualified authority.

Do fixed ladders need cages or ladder safety systems?

Fixed ladder fall protection requirements depend on ladder height, installation date, workplace use, applicable OSHA standards, building code requirements, manufacturer requirements, and project specifications. Kings Mobile Welding installs contractor-directed ladder systems, but the responsible party should verify OSHA, building code, manufacturer, engineer, owner, and inspection requirements before installation.

Do you engineer fixed ladder details?

No. Kings Mobile Welding performs contractor-directed and owner-directed installation and welding work. When ladder installation affects structural capacity, fall protection, life safety, roof access, code-controlled work, anchorage, building envelope, or engineered systems, the detail should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, manufacturer, building owner, inspector, project specifications, OSHA consultant, or qualified authority.

What do you need to quote a fixed ladder installation?

Send the building address, photos of the wall and roof access point, required ladder height, roof or platform access details, wall construction, parapet height, ladder drawings or manufacturer cut sheet, anchorage requirements, access restrictions, work hours, lift requirements, and any OSHA, building code, owner, engineer, manufacturer, or inspection requirements.
