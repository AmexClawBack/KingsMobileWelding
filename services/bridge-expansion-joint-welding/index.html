Bridge Expansion Joint Welding FAQ

What is an elastomeric expansion dam?

An elastomeric expansion dam is a flexible bridge joint system designed to allow bridge sections to expand, contract, and move under traffic loads while sealing the opening between bridge sections. Many systems include steel edge rails, armor angles, or embedded steel that require accurate field welding and fit-up before the elastomer is installed.

Do you perform AWS D1.5 bridge expansion joint welding?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides AWS D1.5 bridge welding support for bridge expansion joint systems, elastomeric expansion dam support steel, structural bridge steel, and inspection-driven maintenance scopes where certified bridge welding is required.

What type of welding do you perform on bridge expansion joint work?

Typical scopes include welding steel edge rails, armor angles, embedded steel, fit-up corrections, build-up welding, repair plates, field modifications, and repair or replacement of damaged joint steel. Final scope depends on the drawings, joint system details, and site conditions.

Why does expansion joint welding matter so much on bridges?

Bridge expansion joints take constant abuse from traffic, weather, and movement. Poor welding around edge rails and joint steel can contribute to premature joint failure, water intrusion, deck deterioration, and repeated maintenance problems.

Is bridge expansion joint replacement recurring maintenance work?

Yes. Bridge expansion joint repair and replacement is a common recurring maintenance scope on DOT and bridge rehabilitation contracts, especially where superstructure maintenance is part of an ongoing program.

What do you need to quote bridge expansion joint welding?

Send drawings or marked-up details, photos if available, project location, traffic control requirements, quantity of work, required welding certifications, inspection requirements, and desired schedule. Start on our contact page.
