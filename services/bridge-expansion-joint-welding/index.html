Bridge Expansion Joint Welding FAQ

What is an elastomeric expansion dam?

An elastomeric expansion dam is a flexible bridge joint system designed to allow bridge sections to expand, contract, and move under traffic loads while sealing the opening between sections. Many systems include steel edge rails or armor angles that require accurate field welding and fit-up before the elastomer is installed.

What type of welding do you perform on bridge expansion joint work?

Typical scopes can include welding steel edge rails, armor angles, embedded steel, fit-up corrections, build-up welding, and repair or replacement of damaged joint steel. Final scope depends on the drawings, joint system details, and site conditions.

Why does expansion joint welding matter so much on bridges?

These joints take constant abuse from traffic and weather. Poor welding can contribute to premature joint failure, water intrusion, and deck damage, which is why joint replacement and maintenance work is closely watched on DOT bridge projects.

Is this recurring bridge maintenance work?

Yes. Bridge expansion joint replacement is one of the most common recurring maintenance scopes on DOT and bridge rehabilitation contracts, especially where superstructure maintenance is a continuing program.

Do you perform AWS D1.5 certified bridge welding?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides AWS D1.5 certified bridge welding for bridge expansion joint systems, structural bridge steel, and inspection-driven maintenance scopes where certified bridge welding is required by project specifications.
