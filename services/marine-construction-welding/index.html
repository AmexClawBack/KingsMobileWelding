Built for Welding Support, Not Full Marine Contracting

Kings Mobile Welding supports marine contractors, GCs, pile driving contractors, ports, terminals, municipalities, industrial waterfront facilities, and construction teams with the welding scope tied to their project.

We are not positioning ourselves as the full marine contractor responsible for engineering, permitting, diving, demolition, pile driving, dredging, or complete dock construction. Our role is field welding, repair welding, reinforcement, structural tie-ins, field modifications, and subcontractor support.

  • Contractor-directed welding: we work from drawings, repair details, owner requirements, engineer details, or contractor direction.
  • Field modifications: support steel, brackets, plates, braces, caps, pile-related steel, access steel, and repair components.
  • Repair support: corrosion damage, impact damage, failed steel, access steel repair, and schedule-critical welding support.
  • Documentation: COI, W-9, safety information, certification information, procurement details, and capability information available when required.

What to Send for a Marine Construction Welding Quote

Marine and waterfront jobs usually need more jobsite context than a basic welding repair. Photos, drawings, access notes, and work windows help us determine whether the scope is a fit and what equipment or support may be required.

For structural capacity, engineered repairs, pile capacity, temporary works, marine structure design, permitting, diving, or code-controlled work, the responsible engineer, marine contractor, owner, inspector, DOT authority, or qualified authority should verify the final repair or installation requirements.

Send These Details

  • Project location, site contact, owner/GC/marine contractor, and required start date
  • Photos of the damaged steel, work area, waterline, access route, dock, pier, pile, bulkhead, or cofferdam
  • Drawings, repair details, splice details, sheet pile layout, pile schedule, weld symbols, or marked-up plans
  • Pipe OD, wall thickness, H-pile size, sheet pile type, plate thickness, bracket sizes, or support steel details
  • Access restrictions, lift/crane access, barge access, tide restrictions, marine safety rules, parking, and work hours
  • Inspection, AWS, DOT, owner, safety, environmental, procurement, COI, W-9, or certification requirements

Marine Construction Welding FAQ

Do you provide marine construction welding?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides marine construction welding support for marine contractors, pile driving contractors, waterfront construction teams, ports, terminals, industrial waterfront facilities, docks, piers, bulkheads, seawalls, cofferdams, sheet pile systems, fender systems, dolphins, access steel, and emergency waterfront repair work.

Do you perform underwater welding or diving work?

No. Kings Mobile Welding provides field welding support for marine and waterfront construction. Underwater welding, diving, engineering, permitting, and full marine construction scopes should be handled by the responsible marine contractor, engineer, owner, diver, or qualified authority when required.

What marine welding services do you support?

We support cofferdam welding, sheet pile welding, marine pile splice welding, pipe pile welding, H-pile welding, bulkhead welding repair, seawall welding repair, dock welding repair, pier welding repair, fender system welding, dolphin repair welding, port welding support, terminal welding support, marine access steel welding, and emergency marine repair welding.

Can you support pile driving and deep foundation contractors on marine projects?

Yes. We support pile driving contractors and deep foundation contractors with marine pile splice welding, pipe pile splice welding, H-pile splice welding, pile caps, pile bracing, templates, support steel, casing, and heavy civil field welding support.

Can you help with dock, pier, bulkhead, and seawall repair welding?

Yes. We can review dock, pier, bulkhead, seawall, and waterfront repair welding scopes when the work involves steel repair, support steel, brackets, plates, reinforcement, impact damage, corrosion-related steel work, access steel, or contractor-directed field welding.

Do you engineer marine repair details?

No. Kings Mobile Welding performs contractor-directed and owner-directed welding. When welding affects structural capacity, pile capacity, bulkhead integrity, seawall performance, cofferdam bracing, temporary works, marine structure design, fall protection, code-controlled work, DOT requirements, life safety, or engineered systems, the detail should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, marine contractor, owner, inspector, DOT authority, or qualified authority.

What should I send for a marine welding quote?

Send drawings, photos, site location, access conditions, water or tide restrictions, pile size, pipe OD, wall thickness, sheet pile details, cofferdam bracing details, repair photos, inspection requirements, work window, safety requirements, and any contractor onboarding documents needed.

Need Marine Construction Welding Support?

Send drawings, repair photos, pile details, sheet pile details, cofferdam bracing details, access notes, inspection requirements, project location, and schedule. We’ll review the marine welding scope and confirm availability.
