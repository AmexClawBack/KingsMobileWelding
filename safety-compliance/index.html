Safety Focus Areas for Mobile Welding Work

Each project has its own safety requirements. These are the common areas we coordinate around before and during mobile welding work.

Hot Work Controls

Permit procedure awareness, spark and slag control, fire watch coordination when required, combustible material awareness, and clean work area setup.

Site Access & Staging

Coordination around lifts, forklifts, cranes, scaffolding, barge access, traffic control, loading docks, tight spaces, occupied facilities, and active jobsites.

PPE & Jobsite Rules

Alignment with site PPE requirements, contractor safety rules, facility policies, owner requirements, and site-specific access procedures.

Pre-Task Planning

Review of drawings, photos, hazards, access, sequencing, material thickness, weld requirements, and field conditions before work begins.

Housekeeping & Area Protection

Clean setup, controlled grinding/cutting, spark protection, finished surface protection, safe teardown, and practical housekeeping around the work zone.

Inspection & Closeout

Coordination around inspection points, punch-list work, repair photos, documentation handoff, and communication with PMs, supers, QC, QA, and inspectors.

Safe Execution on Active Construction, Facility & Infrastructure Sites

Our work often happens around active crews, occupied buildings, operating plants, public infrastructure, traffic areas, finished surfaces, and schedule-sensitive contractor scopes. Safety has to be part of the work plan from the beginning — not an afterthought.

  • Pre-task planning to confirm work area, access, hazards, staging, sequencing, and project-specific requirements
  • Area control using barricades, signage, spark containment, fire watch coordination, and housekeeping practices when required
  • Lift and access coordination around aerial lifts, forklifts, scaffolding, ladders, cranes, barges, lane closures, and active operations
  • Occupied and sensitive environment awareness for retail, commercial, public facilities, plants, warehouses, and finished spaces
  • Clear communication with superintendents, foremen, safety managers, facility teams, inspectors, and project managers

Safety & Compliance FAQ

Are you insured?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding is fully insured and can provide a certificate of insurance when required for onboarding, procurement, project setup, or mobilization.

Do you support hot work permit requirements?

Yes. We coordinate with the controlling contractor or facility and follow the project-specific hot work permit process, fire watch requirements when required, area protection rules, and site safety procedures.

Do you have OSHA 10?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding has OSHA 10 safety training and works with contractors and facilities that require jobsite safety coordination.

Can you provide safety documentation for onboarding?

Yes. We can provide safety information, COI, W-9 upon request, capability information, certification information, UEI, CAGE, eVA ID, SWaM details, and SAM.gov information when required.

Are you certified for structural and bridge welding?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding supports AWS D1.1 structural welding and AWS D1.5 bridge welding requirements for qualifying scopes where the project requires certified welding.

Do you support JHA, AHA, or pre-task safety planning?

Yes. When required by the controlling contractor or facility, we can participate in JHA/AHA processes, toolbox talks, pre-task planning, site safety orientations, and project-specific safety coordination.

What should I send for safety or compliance review?

Send your project location, scope, drawings or photos, site safety requirements, required documents, hot work requirements, onboarding forms, access restrictions, and target schedule through our contact page.

What related pages should our team review?

Teams should review our Procurement, Government & DOT Welding, Welding Subcontractor Services, Structural Steel Welding, and Emergency Welding pages.

Need a Safety Packet, COI, or Onboarding Documents?

Send your site requirements, onboarding forms, project scope, location, and schedule. We will review the request and provide the safety or compliance information needed for mobilization.
