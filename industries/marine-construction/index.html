Common Marine Construction Welding Scopes

Most marine welding scopes are connected to one of three things: keeping production moving, repairing damaged waterfront steel, or modifying field conditions so the next phase of work can continue.

  • Cofferdams: walers, struts, bracing, temporary works steel, tie-ins, welded attachments, field modifications, and repair welding.
  • Sheet pile and bulkheads: cap plates, reinforcement plates, sheet pile attachments, seawall steel, bulkhead steel, brackets, and repair support.
  • Marine pile work: pipe pile splices, H-pile splices, dock pile, pier pile, bridge pile, pile caps, bracing, templates, and pile driving support.
  • Dock and pier repairs: access steel, handrails, ladders, guardrails, platforms, brackets, frames, fender support steel, and damaged steel repair.
  • Fender systems and dolphins: fender brackets, support steel, breasting dolphin repair, mooring dolphin repair, wharf protection steel, and impact damage repairs.
  • Ports and terminals: terminal access steel, loading area steel, wharf steel, fender support steel, brackets, platforms, ladders, and emergency repairs.

Important Marine Welding Planning Notes

Marine construction welding may involve structural capacity, pile capacity, temporary works, fall protection, retaining systems, berthing loads, DOT requirements, owner requirements, inspection requirements, hot work permits, and engineered repair details.

  • When welding affects structural capacity, pile capacity, bulkhead integrity, seawall performance, fall protection, marine structure design, DOT requirements, life safety, or code-controlled work, the detail should be reviewed by the responsible engineer, marine contractor, owner, inspector, safety consultant, DOT authority, or qualified authority.
  • Waterline, tides, corrosion, marine exposure, weather, active facility operations, crane sequencing, and access conditions can affect how the work is planned.
  • Some projects may require owner approval, hot work permitting, terminal badging, site-specific safety training, inspection, WPS documentation, or engineered repair drawings.
  • Kings Mobile Welding performs contractor-directed and owner-directed field welding support, not full engineering, permitting, diving, or complete marine construction.

What to Send for a Marine Construction Welding Quote

The fastest way to get accurate pricing is to send the project information your estimator, superintendent, foreman, or project manager already has. Photos and access notes matter just as much as the weld details.

For marine projects, include whether the work is on land, over water, inside a cofferdam, near a bulkhead, on a dock, at a terminal, or tied to pile driving operations.

Send These Details

  • Project location, contractor name, site contact, facility name, required start date, and work window
  • Photos of the work area, damaged steel, access route, waterline, dock/pier/wharf layout, lift location, crane location, or surrounding conditions
  • Drawings, repair sketches, weld symbols, engineer details, owner requirements, DOT requirements, terminal requirements, or marked-up photos
  • Steel sizes, pipe OD, wall thickness, H-pile size, plate thickness, bracket dimensions, ladder/platform details, or repair component details if known
  • Scope type: cofferdam, sheet pile, bulkhead, dock, pier, pile splice, fender system, dolphin, port, terminal, ladder, handrail, platform, or emergency repair
  • Access restrictions, lift/crane access, barge access, tide restrictions, parking, hot work rules, work hours, security, badging, and marine safety rules
  • Inspection requirements, AWS requirements, WPS requirements, DOT requirements, hold points, safety documentation, COI, W-9, and onboarding requirements
