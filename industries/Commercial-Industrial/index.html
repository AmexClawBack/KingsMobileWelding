Where Welding Shows Up on Commercial & Industrial Jobs

Most commercial and industrial welding calls are field-driven. Contractors and facility teams usually call when a steel detail needs to be finished, damaged steel needs repair, equipment needs support, or downtime needs to be avoided.

  • Commercial construction: stairs, handrails, platforms, embeds, bollards, guards, frames, and misc. metals
  • Warehouses: dock repairs, rack protection, guardrail, bollards, mezzanine repairs, door frames, and safety steel
  • Manufacturing plants: equipment supports, conveyor supports, brackets, frames, machine guards, and repair welding
  • Industrial facilities: catwalks, platforms, ladders, pipe supports, access steel, and structural repair work
  • Facility maintenance: failed weld repairs, cracked steel, worn brackets, impact damage, and field modifications
  • Emergency response: repair welding when damaged steel creates downtime, access issues, or safety concerns

Commercial & Industrial Welding FAQ

Do you provide commercial welding for general contractors?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding works as a welding subcontractor for general contractors and commercial construction teams that need structural steel welding, stairs, handrails, platforms, embeds, misc. metals, field modifications, and punch-list support.

Do you provide industrial welding for active facilities?

Yes. We support active warehouses, manufacturing plants, industrial facilities, and facility maintenance teams with mobile welding, repair welding, equipment support welding, access steel, guards, platforms, and emergency repairs.

Are your welders AWS D1.1 certified?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding provides AWS D1.1 structural welding support for commercial and industrial projects that require qualified welders, proper fit-up, and inspection-ready workmanship.

Can you handle emergency industrial welding?

Yes. We provide emergency and schedule-critical welding support when cracked steel, broken supports, failed welds, impact damage, dock damage, or equipment support issues threaten safety, access, or operations.

Can you work around active operations or after-hours schedules?

Yes. We can coordinate around shutdown windows, production schedules, access restrictions, hot work permits, after-hours needs, and site-specific safety requirements when those constraints are provided up front.

What areas do you cover for commercial and industrial welding?

We support commercial and industrial welding projects across Hampton Roads, Eastern Virginia, and the Mid-Atlantic, including Newport News, Hampton, Norfolk, Portsmouth, Chesapeake, Virginia Beach, Suffolk, Williamsburg, and nearby areas.
