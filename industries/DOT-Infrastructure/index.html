Where Welding Shows Up on Infrastructure Jobs

Most DOT and bridge welding calls are not simple shop fabrication. They are field-driven repairs, production support, and schedule-critical fixes that keep inspections and crews moving.

  • Pile splice welding: driven pile splices, pipe pile splices, H-pile splices, splice plates, and weld-out coordination
  • Bridge repairs: cracked steel, corroded members, reinforcement plates, gussets, brackets, stiffeners, and repair details
  • Structural tie-ins: connection welding, embeds, clip angles, access steel, stairs, platforms, and field modifications
  • Schedule saves: emergency welding, rework correction, punch-list items, and fast mobilization when production is at risk

DOT & Infrastructure Welding FAQ

Do you provide AWS D1.5 bridge welding?

Yes. We support bridge contractors and DOT project teams with AWS D1.5 bridge welding when specified, along with AWS D1.1 structural field welding for related steel scopes.

Do you work as a welding subcontractor for DOT contractors?

Yes. Kings Mobile Welding works as a mobile welding subcontractor for DOT primes, bridge contractors, pile driving contractors, marine contractors, and infrastructure project teams that need certified field welding support.

Can you support pile splice welding?

Yes. We support pipe pile splices, H-pile splices, splice plates, permanent casing splices, pile bracing, pile cap attachments, and related field welding scopes when project specs and access requirements are provided.

Can you mobilize for emergency welding or schedule-critical repairs?

Yes. We provide emergency and schedule-critical welding support for failed welds, rework corrections, punch-list items, damaged steel, and field modifications that need to be handled quickly.

What areas do you cover for bridge and infrastructure welding?

We support projects across Virginia, Maryland, D.C., North Carolina, South Carolina, Hampton Roads, Eastern Virginia, and the Mid-Atlantic. Start with our state pages for Virginia, Maryland, D.C., North Carolina, and South Carolina.
