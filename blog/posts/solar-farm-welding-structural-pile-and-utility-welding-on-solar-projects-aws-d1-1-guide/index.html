Solar pile welding: the structural foundation of solar farms

Why pile welding is required on solar projects

Solar farms rely on driven steel piles or posts to support racking systems. Welding is often required when:

  • piles must be spliced due to depth requirements
  • piles are damaged during driving
  • alignment corrections are needed
  • brackets or connection plates must be added
  • reinforcement is required due to engineering changes

These piles are structural elements. Improper welding can lead to:

  • structural instability
  • inspection failures
  • long-term fatigue cracking
  • corrosion and premature failure
  • liability for EPCs and contractors

This is why most solar projects require welding performed under AWS D1.1 structural welding standards.

Torque tube and racking system welding

Modern solar farms rely heavily on torque tubes and racking systems to support panel arrays. These systems are engineered to precise tolerances, but real-world site conditions often require field modifications.

Common torque tube and racking welding tasks include:

  • crack repairs and reinforcement plates
  • bracket replacement
  • connection repairs
  • misalignment corrections
  • added tabs and plates
  • field retrofits and modifications
