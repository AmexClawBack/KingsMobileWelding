What AWS D1.5 Bridge Welding Means for Hampton Roads Contractors

AWS D1.5 is commonly associated with bridge welding and transportation infrastructure work when required by the project specifications. Bridge welding can involve stricter control over welding procedures, welder qualifications, inspection, testing, documentation, and repair requirements than general field welding.

In Hampton Roads, that matters because bridge and infrastructure work may involve active traffic, water crossings, railroad work, marine access, industrial waterfront environments, night work, lane closures, and tight contractor schedules.

Contractors may need AWS D1.5 bridge welding support for:

  • Bridge rehabilitation welding
  • Bridge repair and structural remediation
  • Expansion joint welding and replacement support
  • Steel reinforcement plates, stiffeners, diaphragms, and support steel
  • DOT or owner-controlled infrastructure welding
  • Railroad bridge and trestle welding support
  • Emergency bridge or infrastructure repair welding

Bridge Welding and Structural Repairs

Bridge welding and structural repair welding can include steel reinforcement, repair plates, modified connections, support steel, diaphragms, stiffeners, damaged components, and remediation work tied to an engineered detail or project specification.

For bridge repair welding, the contractor should confirm the applicable welding code, inspection requirements, material requirements, preparation requirements, preheat requirements, WPS requirements, and whether the repair detail has been reviewed by the responsible engineer or qualified authority.

Related service pages: bridge welding, bridge welding remediation, and structural steel welding.

Expansion Joint Welding and Replacement Support

Expansion joints are schedule-sensitive components because the work may be tied to lane closures, bridge deck repairs, phased traffic control, or owner-controlled work windows. Contractors need welding support that can coordinate with the project schedule and understand the importance of fit-up, access, and inspection.

For expansion joint welding support, send the joint drawings, weld symbols, work window, access notes, required start time, inspection requirements, and any DOT or owner-specific requirements.

Related page: bridge expansion joint welding.

What to Send Before Requesting a Quote

The fastest way to confirm pricing and availability is to send enough information for the welding contractor to understand the steel, access, schedule, inspection requirements, and project conditions.

Send these details when possible:

  • Project location, bridge name, project name, contractor name, and site contact
  • Drawings, repair details, weld symbols, notes, and marked-up plans
  • Photos of the work area, access route, damaged steel, surrounding structure, and equipment access
  • Whether AWS D1.5, AWS D1.1, DOT specifications, owner specifications, WPS, or project-specific requirements apply
  • Inspection requirements, hold points, NDT requirements, preheat requirements, coating removal, or testing requirements
  • Lane closure, traffic control, work window, night work, lift access, crane access, railroad access, marine access, or restricted access notes
  • COI, W-9, certification information, safety information, badging, onboarding, or procurement requirements
