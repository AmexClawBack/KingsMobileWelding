How We Decide Whether a Project Is a Fit

For local repairs, we can often mobilize quickly. For out-of-area work, the best fit is usually contractor-driven work where certified welding, inspection readiness, and schedule support matter.

Best-Fit Travel Scopes

  • Bridge and DOT welding: AWS D1.5 support, repair welding, expansion joints, bridge remediation, and infrastructure steel
  • Pile and deep foundations: H-pile splices, pipe pile splices, permanent casing, cofferdam support, and sheet pile welding
  • Commercial and industrial: facility repairs, plant steel, shutdown work, equipment supports, frames, brackets, rails, and platforms
  • Subcontractor support: schedule-driven welding capacity for primes, GCs, steel fabricators, DOT contractors, and project teams

What to Send With the Location

  • Project address, city, state, site contact, and target schedule
  • Photos, drawings, weld symbols, specs, or repair details
  • Required certifications such as AWS D1.1, AWS D1.5, DOT, owner, or project-specific requirements
  • Access notes including lifts, cranes, lane closures, barge access, shutdown windows, hot work requirements, and site onboarding
