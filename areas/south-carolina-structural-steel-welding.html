South Carolina Structural Steel Welding FAQ

Do you provide AWS D1.1 structural steel welding in South Carolina?

Do you have a main structural steel welding service page?

Can you support steel joist repair and reinforcement?

Yes. We support engineer-directed steel joist repairs, bar joist reinforcement, reinforcement angles, plates, chord repairs, web member repairs, and commercial building modifications. See our Steel Joist Repair & Reinforcement Welding page for more details.

Do you repair beams, columns, and structural steel members?

Yes. We support beam repair welding, column repair welding, HSS repairs, W-shape repairs, reinforcement plates, stiffeners, baseplate work, and approved repair details. See our Beam & Column Repair Welding page for more details.

Can you weld field connections and structural steel splices?

Yes. We support field-welded structural steel connections, beam splices, column splices, welded plates, brackets, clip angles, shear tabs, field corrections, and contractor-directed structural welding. See our Field-Welded Structural Steel Connections & Splices page.

Can you support embed plates, base plates, anchor rods, steel deck, and shear studs?

Yes. We support embed plate welding, base plate welding, anchor rod-related welding support, steel deck welding, shear stud welding support, weld washers, deck repairs, and related commercial construction welding scopes where welding is shown on approved drawings or directed by the project team.

Do you engineer structural steel repairs?

No. We perform the welding portion of contractor-directed and engineer-directed structural repairs based on approved drawings, weld symbols, repair details, project specifications, or field instructions. Engineering, design responsibility, and final repair details should come from the project engineer or responsible design professional.
